# üìñ The Saga of Indian Democracy: The Making of the Constitution

## üìú Chapter 1: The Foundation ‚Äì What is a Rulebook?

Before India could be free, it needed a soul‚Äîa document that defined its existence. This is the **Constitution**.
*   **The Concept:** A Constitution isn't just a book; it lays out the "ideals" a nation aspires to. It defines the structure of the state and the rights of the people.
*   **The Global Context:** The tradition of a written constitution began with the **USA** in **1787** (following their independence on July 4, 1776).
*   **The Democracy Paradox:** While every democracy likely has a constitution, having a constitution doesn't make a country democratic.
    *   *Example:* **China** (Communist), **North Korea** (Totalitarian), and **Iran** (Oligarchic - rule by a few) all have constitutions but are not true democracies like India or the USA.
    *   **Monarchic Systems:** Countries like the **UK** and **Saudi Arabia** are monarchies. The UK is unique because it has an **"Unwritten Constitution"**‚Äîthey never sat down to write a single document; they rely on centuries of laws and conventions. India, conversely, has the largest **written** constitution.

## ‚úä Chapter 2: The Struggle for the Pen (1928‚Äì1946)

For centuries, the British wrote laws for India. The demand for Indians to write their own laws was a slow-burning fire.

1.  **1928 (The Nehru Report):** Stung by the British claim that Indians couldn't agree on anything, **Motilal Nehru** (father of Jawaharlal Nehru) chaired an All Parties Conference and drafted the first blueprint for a constitution.
2.  **1934 (The Inception):** The formal idea of a **"Constituent Assembly"** (a body elected specifically to draft the constitution) was first proposed by **M.N. Roy**, a pioneer of the Communist movement in India.
3.  **1935 (Official Demand):** The **Indian National Congress (INC)** officially adopted this demand.
4.  **1940 (The August Offer):** Desperate for Indian support in World War II, Viceroy Linlithgow offered **"Dominion Status"** (India rules itself, but under the British Crown). Congress rejected this; they wanted full independence.
5.  **1942 (The Cripps Mission):** Sir Stafford Cripps came with a draft proposal for after the war. Mahatma Gandhi rejected it as a **"post-dated cheque on a crashing bank."**
6.  **1946 (The Breakthrough - Cabinet Mission Plan):** Three British Cabinet members arrived:
    *   **Lord Pethick Lawrence** (Chairman)
    *   **Sir Stafford Cripps**
    *   **A.V. Alexander**
    *   *The Plan:* They rejected the Muslim League's demand for two separate assemblies (one for Pakistan) but proposed a plan that convinced both parties to join one Constituent Assembly.

## üèõÔ∏è Chapter 3: Designing the Assembly (The 389 Seats)

The Cabinet Mission Plan laid out the architecture of the **Constituent Assembly (CA)** based on the population (approx. **1 seat per 1 Million people**).

### **The Grand Total: 389 Seats**
The Assembly was a hybrid body: **Partly Elected and Partly Nominated.**

**A. British India (296 Seats):**
These were areas directly ruled by the British.
*   **292 Seats:** From the 11 Governor's Provinces (e.g., Madras, Bombay, UP, Bihar).
*   **4 Seats:** From the 4 Chief Commissioner's Provinces (Think of these as the Union Territories of that time).
    *   *Mnemonic 'ABCD':* **A**jmer-Merwara, **B**ritish Baluchistan, **C**oorg, **D**elhi.
*   **Election Method:** **Indirect Election.** The common people did *not* vote. The members of the *Provincial Legislative Assemblies* (state MLAs) elected these representatives using **Proportional Representation by means of Single Transferable Vote**.
*   **Seat Allocation:** Seats were divided among three communities: **Muslims, Sikhs, and General** (everyone else).

**B. Princely States (93 Seats):**
These were ruled by Indian Kings/Nawabs.
*   **Method:** **Nomination.** The heads of these states (Kings) simply handpicked their representatives. There was no election here.

### **The Election Results (July-Aug 1946)**
*   **Indian National Congress:** Won **208** seats (Massive majority).
*   **Muslim League:** Won **73** seats.
*   **Independents/Small Groups:** Won **15** seats.
*   *Note:* The 93 Princely State seats remained empty initially because the Kings decided to stay away, hoping to remain independent kingdoms.

While the Constitution was being written, who ran the country? The British set up a temporary **Interim Government** on **September 2, 1946**.
*   **Structure:** The Viceroy remained the Head. **Jawaharlal Nehru** was the **Vice-President** of the Executive Council (acting as Prime Minister).
*   **The Muslim League Twist:** They initially boycotted it but joined in October 1946 to obstruct Congress from within.

## üèóÔ∏è Chapter 5: The Assembly Begins Work (Important Dates)

**1. The First Meeting: Dec 9, 1946**
*   **Attendance:** Only **211 members** attended. The Muslim League boycotted, demanding Pakistan.
*   **The President:** Following the *French practice* of choosing the oldest member, **Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha** was elected as the **Temporary President**.

**2. The Second Meeting: Dec 11, 1946**
*   **Dr. Rajendra Prasad** was elected as the **Permanent President**.
*   **H.C. Mukherjee** and **V.T. Krishnamachari** were elected as **Vice-Presidents**.
*   **Sir B.N. Rau** was appointed as the **Constitutional Advisor** (He was the legal genius who prepared the initial rough draft for the drafting committee).

**3. The Vision: Dec 13, 1946**
*   **Jawaharlal Nehru** moved the historic **"Objective Resolution"**.
*   It outlined the philosophy of the constitution (Justice, Equality, Liberty).
*   **Adopted on:** **January 22, 1947**.
*   *Legacy:* This resolution was later modified to become the **Preamble** of the Constitution.

**4. The Partition Impact (1947)**
*   After the Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947) formalized Partition, the members from Pakistan areas left.
*   **New Strength:** The Assembly reduced to **299 members** (229 British India + 70 Princely States).
*   **Dec 31, 1947:** The membership was 299.

## üèÅ Chapter 7: Enactment and Enforcement

The Constitution went through **three readings** (debates).
*   **1st Reading:** Nov 4-9, 1948.
*   **2nd Reading:** Nov 15, 1948 - Oct 17, 1949 (Longest, clause-by-clause).
*   **3rd Reading:** Nov 14, 1949 - Nov 26, 1949.

### **Critical Date 1: November 26, 1949 (Adoption)**
*   This day is celebrated as **Constitution Day**.
*   The Constitution was **adopted and enacted**.
*   **284 members** were present and signed the document (First signature: Nehru; Last: Rajendra Prasad).
*   **Immediate Effect:** Provisions related to **Citizenship (Arts 5-9), Elections (Art 324), and Provisional Parliament** came into force *immediately* on this date.

### **Critical Date 2: January 26, 1950 (Commencement)**
*   This day is celebrated as **Republic Day**.
*   The **rest** of the Constitution (Fundamental Rights, etc.) came into force.
*   **Why Jan 26?** To honor the **Purna Swaraj** (Complete Independence) resolution celebrated on Jan 26, 1930, following the Lahore Session (1929).

## üß© Chapter 8: Facts, Figures & Symbols

*   **Time Taken:** 2 Years, 11 Months, 18 Days.
*   **Total Sessions:** 11 Sessions (spanning 165 days of actual sitting).
*   **Total Expenditure:** ‚Çπ64 Lakhs.
*   **Symbol (Seal):** **Elephant** (representing the size and diversity).
*   **Women Members:** Total **15** women were part of the CA (e.g., Sarojini Naidu, Hansa Mehta, Rajkumari Amrit Kaur, Sucheta Kriplani).
*   **Chief Draftsman:** **S.N. Mukherjee** (The bureaucrat who put complex thoughts into legal language).
*   **Calligraphers:**
    *   **English:** **Prem Behari Narain Raizada** (wrote it in flowing Italic style).
    *   **Hindi:** **Vasant Krishan Vaidya**.
*   **Artists:** Illuminated/Decorated by artists from Shantiniketan, led by **Nandalal Bose** and **Beohar Rammanohar Sinha**.
*   **Nehru's Speech:** On the midnight of Aug 14-15, 1947, Nehru delivered the famous **"Tryst with Destiny"** speech.
*   **Quote:** Somnath Lahiri (Communist leader) criticized the CA saying, *"The Constituent Assembly was British made and was working the British plan as the British should like it to be worked out."*

### **The Double Role (1947-1949)**
After the Indian Independence Act 1947, the Assembly became a sovereign body with two functions:
1.  **Making Constitution:** Chaired by **Dr. Rajendra Prasad**.
2.  **Legislating (Making Laws):** Chaired by **G.V. Mavalankar** (He became the first Speaker of Lok Sabha).

### **Adoption of National Symbols**
1.  **National Flag:** July 22, 1947.
2.  **Commonwealth Membership Ratified:** May 1949.
3.  **National Anthem (Jana Gana Mana):** January 24, 1950.
4.  **National Song (Vande Mataram):** January 24, 1950.
5.  **First President (Rajendra Prasad):** Elected on January 24, 1950.
